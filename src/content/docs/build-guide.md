This guide walks you through building your keyboard from the moment you open the box to your first keystroke.

## Step 1: Unbox and Inspect

Lay out all your parts on a clean, flat surface. Check the [Parts List](/docs/parts-list) to make sure everything is there.

Look for any obvious damage — cracked PCB, bent pins, missing keycaps. If anything looks off, reach out before you start building.

## Step 2: Test the PCB

Before you build anything, plug the bare PCB into your computer via USB-C. Use a site like [Keyboard Tester](https://www.keyboardtester.com/) and short each switch pad with tweezers or a paperclip. Every key should register.

> **This step is important.** It's much easier to deal with a dead PCB before you've assembled the whole board.

## Step 3: Install Stabilizers

Stabilizers go on **before** switches. They support the larger keys (spacebar, shift, enter, backspace).

1. Clip the stabilizer stems into the housing
2. Snap the stabilizer wire into place
3. Insert into the PCB — they should click in firmly
4. Test by pressing down — they should feel smooth with no rattle

## Step 4: Mount Switches

If you have a hot-swap PCB:

1. Place the plate on top of the PCB
2. Align switch pins with the hot-swap sockets
3. Press firmly until the switch clicks into the plate
4. Start with the corner switches to align the plate, then fill in the rest

Make sure no pins are bent before pressing. Bent pins = dead switch.

## Step 5: Install Keycaps

Press each keycap onto its switch stem. They should fit snugly. Refer to your layout diagram for correct placement.

The spacebar stabilizer wire goes on the **bottom** of the spacebar keycap.

## Step 6: Case Assembly

1. Lower the assembled plate+PCB into the case
2. The friction fit should hold it in place
3. Apply rubber feet to the bottom corners

## Step 7: Connect and Test

Plug in your USB-C cable and test every single key again. This time they should all register with proper keycap labels.

If a key doesn't work:
- Pull the switch and check for bent pins
- Re-seat the switch firmly
- If still dead, try a spare switch

## You're Done

Congrats — you just built a mechanical keyboard. Head to [Firmware](/docs/firmware) if you want to customize your keymap.
